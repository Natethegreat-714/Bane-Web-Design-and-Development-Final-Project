Picture Perfect Trip

Where would you go?

When asked about what my dream vacation spot would be, I always find myself responding with Switzerland. From cities like Geneva and Zürich to the iconic Matterhorn and the alpine town of Zermatt, Switzerland is full of exciting locations for any type of traveler. In terms of historical and architectural landmarks, Switzerland is in a league of its own with Château de Chillon in Montreux serving as just one example of the country’s rich history (see Figure 1). The diverse array of tourist hotspots and local gems make Switzerland one of the best getaway locales, as it truly has something for everyone.

What would you do?

Switzerland offers countless activities for every kind of traveler. Outdoor enthusiasts can take part in mountain adventures like skiing or hiking through the Alps. Visitors can also enjoy relaxing experiences like scenic train rides or cable car tours. Below are just a few highlights:

  • Hiking through alpine trails
  • Skiing at world-class resorts
  • Riding scenic trains like the Glacier Express
  • Exploring Swiss nightlife in Zürich or Geneva
  • Taking boat rides on Lake Geneva

Regardless of the season, Switzerland offers plenty of activities that make you want to return year after year. One event that stands out is the annual hot air balloon festival in Château-d'Oex, which runs for nine days and features a variety of shows and family-friendly activities (see Figure 2).

What would you eat?

Switzerland’s culinary scene is also one of its major selling points to tourists. Due to its unique location in the heart of western Europe, Switzerland pulls a lot from its neighbor countries such as Germany, France, and Italy when it comes to cuisine, leaving visitors with plenty of options to choose from during their stay. Pastries and breads such as Zopf and Gipfeli are key staples of the country as they are fresh and locally made. Traditional local dishes such as the Berner Platte offer a variety of Swiss style sausages, potatoes, and sauerkraut that make it a must try upon visiting (see Figure 3). The country of course is also well known for its chocolates and cheeses. Offering a wide variety of artisanal crafted delicacies that range in both flavors and cost. Tasting businesses are also widely popular in Switzerland as they allow tourists to sample a diverse range of local delicacies during their visit (see Figure 4).
